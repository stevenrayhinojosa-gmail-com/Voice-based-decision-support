Data Collection Guide

Why collect behavioral data?

  • Identify patterns and triggers
  • Measure effectiveness of interventions
  • Train machine learning models
  • Support evidence-based decision making

Tips for quality data entry:

  • Be objective and specific in descriptions
  • Record data as soon as possible after the incident
  • Focus on observable behavior, not interpretations
  • Include context and environmental factors
  • Note any interventions applied and their effects
The more consistent and detailed your data entry, the more effective your analysis and decision support will be.
Data Metrics

Understanding the severity metrics:

1-3: Mild
4-7: Moderate
8-10: Severe
Rate the intensity of the behavior on a scale from 1-10

Frequency: Count of how many times the behavior occurred within the relevant time period (e.g., per day, per session).

Duration: Total time the behavior lasted, measured in minutes.
